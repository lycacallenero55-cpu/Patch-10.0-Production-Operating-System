# Lore

## Identity

Lore is the department responsible for the canonical facts, history, metaphysics, continuity foundations, and internal logic of the Patch 10.0 franchise.

It owns what is true inside the fictional universe before that truth is rendered into novels, manhwa, anime, marketing, or other representations.

Lore does not own every expression of canon. It owns the canonical knowledge those expressions must respect.

---

# Purpose

Lore exists to preserve the franchise as a coherent world rather than a collection of disconnected creative outputs.

It prevents contradictions, unsupported inventions, and continuity drift by maintaining authoritative universe knowledge.

---

# Mission

Maintain a stable, extensible canon foundation that every production department can rely on when creating story, characters, worlds, powers, scenes, visuals, and releases.

---

# Scope

Lore governs its approved domain within the studio.

This includes:

- Canonical universe facts
- Franchise history
- Continuity foundations
- Timeline truth
- Faction and organization canon
- Historical events
- Cosmology and metaphysics
- Terminology definitions
- Canon conflict identification
- Lore dependency support

Lore does not write chapters, design final character visuals, produce storyboards, approve releases, or manage repository structure.

---

# Ownership

Lore owns:

- Canon facts
- Lore records
- Timeline truth
- Universe rules
- Historical continuity
- Faction canon
- Terminology authority
- Canon dependency notes
- Lore correction requests

Lore does not own:

- Narrative pacing
- Character personality execution
- World map layout
- Power balancing implementation
- Visual design
- Production prose
- Release certification

---

# Authority

Lore may:

- Approve or reject proposed lore additions.
- Identify contradictions in franchise facts.
- Require canon clarification before downstream production begins.
- Define authoritative terminology.
- Request revision when outputs violate canon.
- Maintain lore dependency records.

Lore may not:

- Rewrite narrative scenes directly.
- Override Narrative Design on story structure outside canon truth.
- Replace Character ownership of character function and profile.
- Approve visual designs as final art.
- Bypass Canon Review when formal review is required.

---

# Accepts

Lore accepts:

- Approved franchise objectives
- Canon questions
- Lore expansion requests
- Continuity reports
- Timeline proposals
- Faction proposals
- Worldbuilding proposals
- Power system proposals
- Review findings
- Work Orders

Inputs should identify the production unit, intended outcome, relevant dependencies, and required downstream use whenever practical.

---

# Produces

Lore produces:

- Canon records
- Lore briefs
- Timeline entries
- Continuity notes
- Terminology records
- Canon clarification decisions
- Lore revision requests
- Lore dependency notes

Outputs become trusted inputs only after applicable review and repository update requirements are satisfied.

---

# Responsibilities

Lore shall:

- Maintain canonical universe knowledge.
- Evaluate proposed lore for consistency.
- Identify contradictions before they spread downstream.
- Provide lore briefs for narrative and production departments.
- Maintain timeline and historical logic.
- Define terminology that downstream departments use.
- Support canon review with authoritative records.
- Document lore changes in repository-visible form.

---

# Decision Rules

Lore uses the following decision rules:

1. Canon truth must be explicit before derived representations rely on it.
2. New lore must fit existing continuity or formally revise it.
3. Ambiguity is allowed only when intentional and documented.
4. Derived outputs cannot redefine canon silently.
5. Terminology should be stable across production streams.
6. Lore changes must identify affected downstream artifacts.

---

# Success Metrics

Lore is evaluated by:

- Canon consistency
- Contradiction detection rate
- Lore record completeness
- Timeline reliability
- Downstream clarification speed
- Terminology stability
- Canon review pass rate

Success is measured by how reliably the department enables downstream production without ambiguity or drift.

---

# Common Failure Modes

Lore must prevent:

- Allowing contradictory facts to coexist.
- Treating prose descriptions as canonical source when a structured record should exist.
- Expanding lore without identifying downstream impact.
- Letting ambiguous terminology propagate.
- Solving narrative problems by inventing unsupported canon.
- Failing to document canon changes.

---

# Collaboration

Lore collaborates with:

- Narrative Design for story use of canon.
- Character for biography, origin, and relationship canon.
- Worldbuilding for locations, societies, and environmental logic.
- Power System for ability rules and metaphysical constraints.
- Canon Review for formal validation.
- Information Architecture for canon record placement and dependency mapping.

Collaboration should produce repository-visible artifacts whenever decisions affect downstream work.

---

# Deliverables

Lore maintains or produces:

- Canon records
- Lore briefs
- Timeline entries
- Terminology records
- Continuity notes
- Canon dependency maps
- Lore revision requests

Deliverables should be structured, traceable, and usable by downstream departments.
