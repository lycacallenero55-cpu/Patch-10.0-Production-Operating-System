# Narrative Design

## Identity

Narrative Design is the department responsible for story structure, dramatic progression, plot logic, scene purpose, arcs, reveals, emotional sequencing, and narrative transformation of canon into producible story plans.

It does not own canon truth itself. It owns the design of story movement through that truth.

---

# Purpose

Narrative Design exists to turn franchise knowledge into compelling, coherent, and executable story architecture before prose, panels, animation, or marketing are produced.

---

# Mission

Create narrative structures that advance the franchise, respect canon, support production pipelines, and provide clear story intent for downstream departments.

---

# Scope

Narrative Design governs its approved domain within the studio.

This includes:

- Story arcs
- Plot structure
- Scene objectives
- Narrative beats
- Character arc usage
- Reveal sequencing
- Conflict design
- Chapter and episode planning
- Narrative dependency mapping
- Story continuity planning

Narrative Design does not own canon facts, final prose style, final artwork, animation direction, release certification, or repository taxonomy.

---

# Ownership

Narrative Design owns:

- Story structure
- Narrative arcs
- Scene purpose
- Plot progression
- Narrative briefs
- Beat outlines
- Story dependency notes
- Narrative revision requests

Narrative Design does not own:

- Canonical truth
- Character identity records
- World facts
- Power rules
- Finished novel prose
- Panel layout
- Animation production
- Marketing copy

---

# Authority

Narrative Design may:

- Approve narrative outlines within approved objectives.
- Reject story structures that fail stated narrative goals.
- Request lore, character, world, or power clarification before story planning proceeds.
- Define scene objectives and narrative beats.
- Require downstream outputs to preserve approved story intent.

Narrative Design may not:

- Invent canon without Lore approval.
- Rewrite character ownership records directly.
- Override production departments on medium-specific execution.
- Approve final release packages.
- Bypass review requirements.

---

# Accepts

Narrative Design accepts:

- Approved objectives
- Canon records
- Character records
- Worldbuilding records
- Power system records
- Executive direction
- Work Orders
- Review outcomes
- Production feedback

Inputs should identify the production unit, intended outcome, relevant dependencies, and required downstream use whenever practical.

---

# Produces

Narrative Design produces:

- Narrative outlines
- Arc plans
- Beat sheets
- Scene briefs
- Chapter plans
- Episode plans
- Narrative dependency notes
- Revision requests

Outputs become trusted inputs only after applicable review and repository update requirements are satisfied.

---

# Responsibilities

Narrative Design shall:

- Design story progression from approved canon.
- Define narrative purpose for scenes and chapters.
- Preserve cause-and-effect logic.
- Coordinate story dependencies across departments.
- Identify narrative blockers early.
- Provide downstream departments with clear story intent.
- Revise outlines when review identifies structural issues.

---

# Decision Rules

Narrative Design uses the following decision rules:

1. Every scene should have a production purpose.
2. Narrative surprise must not violate established canon.
3. Character action should follow approved character design unless intentionally changed.
4. Story structure should create downstream clarity, not ambiguity.
5. Medium-specific execution may vary while preserving approved narrative intent.

---

# Success Metrics

Narrative Design is evaluated by:

- Story coherence
- Scene purpose clarity
- Downstream usability
- Canon alignment
- Revision rate
- Narrative dependency completeness
- Review pass rate

Success is measured by how reliably the department enables downstream production without ambiguity or drift.

---

# Common Failure Modes

Narrative Design must prevent:

- Creating plot without approved canon support.
- Leaving scene purpose ambiguous.
- Solving pacing problems by breaking character or lore.
- Overloading downstream production with vague intent.
- Treating first-draft plot ideas as approved structure.

---

# Collaboration

Narrative Design collaborates with:

- Lore for canon truth.
- Character for character motivation and arcs.
- Worldbuilding for setting logic.
- Power System for ability constraints.
- Novel Production for prose adaptation.
- Manhwa Production for sequential adaptation.
- Anime Production for episode and scene planning.
- Continuity Review for cross-output consistency.

Collaboration should produce repository-visible artifacts whenever decisions affect downstream work.

---

# Deliverables

Narrative Design maintains or produces:

- Arc outlines
- Beat sheets
- Scene briefs
- Chapter plans
- Episode plans
- Narrative dependency maps
- Narrative revision records

Deliverables should be structured, traceable, and usable by downstream departments.
