# Worldbuilding

## Identity

Worldbuilding is the department responsible for places, cultures, institutions, environments, geography, societies, technology context, and lived-world logic within Patch 10.0.

It owns the systems and settings that make the franchise world feel consistent and producible.

---

# Purpose

Worldbuilding exists to give the franchise a stable environment where lore, characters, narrative, powers, and visuals can operate without contradiction.

---

# Mission

Maintain world records that define how locations, societies, institutions, environments, and material systems function across story and production outputs.

---

# Scope

Worldbuilding governs its approved domain within the studio.

This includes:

- Locations
- Geography
- Cultures
- Institutions
- Factions as social structures
- Environmental rules
- Infrastructure
- Technology context
- Settlement logic
- World dependency notes

Worldbuilding does not own the full canon timeline, character identity, story structure, ability mechanics, final environment art, or release approval.

---

# Ownership

Worldbuilding owns:

- Location records
- Culture records
- Institution records
- Geography notes
- Environmental logic
- World maps as planning artifacts
- Setting briefs
- World continuity corrections

Worldbuilding does not own:

- Plot progression
- Character motivation
- Power rules
- Final illustrated backgrounds
- Animation layouts
- Release packaging
- Repository taxonomy

---

# Authority

Worldbuilding may:

- Approve location, culture, and institution records.
- Reject outputs that misuse approved world logic.
- Request lore or narrative clarification when setting logic depends on it.
- Define environmental constraints for downstream production.
- Maintain world dependency notes.

Worldbuilding may not:

- Change canon history without Lore approval.
- Override Narrative Design on story structure.
- Approve final environment art as finished asset.
- Modify character identity records directly.
- Bypass review.

---

# Accepts

Worldbuilding accepts:

- Approved objectives
- Lore records
- Narrative needs
- Character needs
- Power system needs
- Environment proposals
- Production feedback
- Review findings
- Work Orders

Inputs should identify the production unit, intended outcome, relevant dependencies, and required downstream use whenever practical.

---

# Produces

Worldbuilding produces:

- Location briefs
- Culture briefs
- Institution records
- Setting guides
- Geography notes
- Environmental constraints
- World dependency notes
- World revision requests

Outputs become trusted inputs only after applicable review and repository update requirements are satisfied.

---

# Responsibilities

Worldbuilding shall:

- Maintain coherent setting records.
- Define how societies and places function.
- Support narrative and visual departments with setting context.
- Identify world logic contradictions.
- Document environmental constraints.
- Track setting dependencies across production units.
- Revise world records when approved changes require it.

---

# Decision Rules

Worldbuilding uses the following decision rules:

1. World details should support production use, not only decoration.
2. Locations must have stable names, functions, and relationships.
3. Culture and institution details must not contradict lore.
4. Environmental constraints should be explicit before visual production.
5. Major setting changes must identify affected story and assets.

---

# Success Metrics

Worldbuilding is evaluated by:

- Location record completeness
- Setting consistency
- Downstream usability
- World logic conflict detection
- Environmental constraint clarity
- Review pass rate

Success is measured by how reliably the department enables downstream production without ambiguity or drift.

---

# Common Failure Modes

Worldbuilding must prevent:

- Creating locations without function.
- Letting visual needs invent unapproved setting facts.
- Duplicating location truth across files.
- Ignoring social or environmental consequences.
- Failing to update downstream dependencies after setting changes.

---

# Collaboration

Worldbuilding collaborates with:

- Lore for universe truth and history.
- Narrative Design for story use of settings.
- Character for cultural and relational context.
- Power System for environmental effects on abilities.
- Environment Art for visual translation.
- Continuity Review for setting consistency.

Collaboration should produce repository-visible artifacts whenever decisions affect downstream work.

---

# Deliverables

Worldbuilding maintains or produces:

- Location briefs
- Culture records
- Institution records
- Setting guides
- Geography notes
- Environmental constraint notes
- World dependency maps

Deliverables should be structured, traceable, and usable by downstream departments.
