# Asset Management

## Identity

Asset Management is the department responsible for organizing, tracking, versioning, and preserving production assets and references.

It owns asset records and retrieval discipline, not the creative approval of the assets themselves.

---

# Purpose

Asset Management exists to ensure that production teams can find the correct assets, understand their status, and avoid using outdated or unapproved references.

---

# Mission

Maintain asset organization, metadata, status, provenance, and handoff records so that production assets remain usable across the franchise lifecycle.

---

# Scope

Asset Management governs its approved domain within the studio.

This includes:

- Asset inventories
- Reference libraries
- Asset metadata
- Version records
- Asset status tracking
- Asset handoff packages
- Archive coordination
- Provenance records

Asset Management does not create final art, approve creative quality, define canon, certify releases, or own repository taxonomy standards.

---

# Ownership

Asset Management owns:

- Asset inventory records
- Asset metadata
- Asset status records
- Version tracking
- Reference package organization
- Asset handoff records
- Asset archive coordination

Asset Management does not own:

- Creative approval
- Character art authority
- Environment art authority
- Canon truth
- Marketing approval
- Release certification

---

# Authority

Asset Management may:

- Require metadata before assets are accepted into managed libraries.
- Flag missing, duplicate, outdated, or ambiguous assets.
- Maintain version and status records.
- Route asset approval questions to owning departments.
- Coordinate archive of deprecated assets.

Asset Management may not:

- Approve rejected assets.
- Modify creative content without owner approval.
- Invent asset provenance.
- Delete assets without archive procedure.
- Bypass release or visual review.

---

# Accepts

Asset Management accepts:

- Approved assets
- Reference materials
- Asset update requests
- Version changes
- Production package assets
- Archive requests
- Review feedback

Inputs should identify purpose, source materials, dependencies, required output, and review expectations whenever practical.

---

# Produces

Asset Management produces:

- Asset inventories
- Reference indexes
- Asset metadata records
- Version logs
- Asset handoff packages
- Archive records
- Missing-asset reports

Outputs become trusted inputs only after applicable review and repository update requirements are satisfied.

---

# Responsibilities

Asset Management shall:

- Track asset identity, status, owner, version, and location.
- Prevent outdated asset usage.
- Support production handoffs with correct references.
- Maintain asset provenance and archive trails.
- Report missing or inconsistent asset metadata.
- Coordinate with owning departments for approval status.

---

# Decision Rules

Asset Management uses the following decision rules:

1. An unmanaged asset is not production-ready.
2. Status and owner must be visible.
3. Deprecated assets must not compete with approved references.
4. Version changes require traceability.
5. Asset organization should optimize reliable retrieval.

---

# Success Metrics

Asset Management is evaluated by:

- Asset retrieval reliability
- Metadata completeness
- Outdated asset reduction
- Version accuracy
- Handoff success
- Archive integrity

Success is measured by reliability, traceability, usefulness to downstream departments, and reduction of avoidable production risk.

---

# Common Failure Modes

Asset Management must prevent:

- Using old references by accident.
- Storing assets without owner or status.
- Losing provenance.
- Allowing duplicate assets to become competing sources.
- Treating file storage as asset management.

---

# Collaboration

Asset Management collaborates with:

- Character Art and Environment Art for approved references.
- Manhwa, Anime, and Cinematic Production for asset handoffs.
- Information Architecture for placement rules.
- Release Certification for package completeness.

Collaboration should produce repository-visible artifacts whenever decisions affect downstream work.

---

# Deliverables

Asset Management maintains or produces:

- Asset inventories
- Reference indexes
- Metadata records
- Version logs
- Asset handoff packages
- Archive records
- Missing-asset reports

Deliverables should be structured, reviewable, and traceable to the work they support.

# Closing Statement

Asset Management protects production memory for visual and reference materials.

It ensures that teams use the right asset, in the right version, for the right purpose.
