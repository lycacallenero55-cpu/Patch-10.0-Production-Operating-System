# Environment Art

## Identity

Environment Art is the department responsible for visual interpretation of approved worldbuilding into location concepts, environment references, background standards, and setting visual guides.

It owns visual environment references, not the underlying world canon.

---

# Purpose

Environment Art exists to make locations and settings visually coherent, producible, and consistent across story, manhwa, anime, cinematic, and marketing workflows.

---

# Mission

Produce environment visual references that preserve approved setting logic, support production needs, and prevent visual drift across outputs.

---

# Scope

Environment Art governs its approved production domain.

This includes:

- Location concept briefs
- Environment references
- Background standards
- Setting visual guides
- Prop and infrastructure references
- Lighting and mood references
- Environment revision packages
- Cross-medium setting consistency

Environment Art does not own world canon, geography truth, story outcomes, final panel composition, final animation timing, or release certification.

---

# Ownership

Environment Art owns:

- Environment visual references
- Location concept sheets
- Background standards
- Setting visual guides
- Environment art revision records
- Visual setting consistency notes

Environment Art does not own:

- Worldbuilding records
- Canon history
- Character identity
- Storyboard camera planning
- Final release packaging
- Marketing strategy

---

# Authority

Environment Art may:

- Create environment references from approved worldbuilding.
- Reject visual outputs that violate approved environment standards.
- Request worldbuilding clarification before design.
- Revise environment references after review.
- Maintain setting variant notes for different production streams.

Environment Art may not:

- Invent unapproved setting facts through visuals.
- Change geography or institution logic directly.
- Override story or storyboard requirements outside environment scope.
- Approve final releases.
- Bypass visual review.

---

# Accepts

Environment Art accepts:

- Worldbuilding records
- Narrative briefs
- Storyboard needs
- Manhwa and anime requirements
- Cinematic requirements
- Production requests
- Visual review feedback

Inputs should identify source canon, approved narrative intent, production unit, required output format, and acceptance criteria whenever practical.

---

# Produces

Environment Art produces:

- Location concept sheets
- Environment reference packs
- Background standards
- Setting visual guides
- Prop references
- Lighting and mood notes
- Revision packages

Outputs become official only after applicable review, repository update, and release-readiness requirements are satisfied.

---

# Responsibilities

Environment Art shall:

- Translate approved setting records into visual references.
- Maintain environmental consistency across production streams.
- Support downstream art, storyboard, and cinematic workflows.
- Identify conflicts between visuals and world logic.
- Document approved variants and revisions.
- Prepare environment references for review and asset management.

---

# Decision Rules

Environment Art uses the following decision rules:

1. Environment visuals must preserve approved setting logic.
2. Readable production references have priority over decorative ambiguity.
3. Visual mood should support scene purpose and world identity.
4. Design variants must remain traceable to approved locations.
5. Environment details must not create unapproved canon.

---

# Success Metrics

Environment Art is evaluated by:

- Reference completeness
- Setting consistency
- Downstream usability
- Visual review pass rate
- Revision efficiency
- Cross-medium environment coherence

Success is measured by production-ready outputs that preserve canon, intent, medium fit, and downstream usability.

---

# Common Failure Modes

Environment Art must prevent:

- Adding visual details that contradict worldbuilding.
- Making environments beautiful but unusable for production.
- Failing to document location variants.
- Letting backgrounds drift between media.
- Ignoring storyboard or scene requirements.

---

# Collaboration

Environment Art collaborates with:

- Worldbuilding for setting truth.
- Storyboard for spatial and camera needs.
- Manhwa Production and Anime Production for medium requirements.
- Cinematic Production for lighting and mood needs.
- Visual Review for quality validation.
- Asset Management for reference storage.

Collaboration should produce repository-visible artifacts whenever decisions affect downstream work.

---

# Deliverables

Environment Art maintains or produces:

- Location concepts
- Environment reference packs
- Background standards
- Setting visual guides
- Prop and infrastructure references
- Environment revision records

Deliverables should be structured, reviewable, and usable by downstream production and release workflows.
